# Apple App Store Review Guidelines 2025

## Critical Technical Requirements (2025)
- **Build with Xcode 16 or later** (mandatory starting April 24, 2025)
- **Use iOS 18/iPadOS 18 SDK** or later versions
- **No crashes or bugs** - thoroughly test on latest iOS devices
- **Complete functionality** - no placeholder content or broken features

## Core Review Categories

### 1. Safety (Most Important)
- Content moderation for user-generated content
- No offensive, discriminatory, or harmful material
- Child safety protections
- No apps that could cause physical harm

### 2. Performance
- App completeness (40% of rejections are here)
- Accurate metadata and descriptions
- Energy efficiency
- Use only public APIs

### 3. Business Model
- Use in-app purchases for digital content
- Transparent subscription terms
- Clear pricing
- Respect intellectual property

### 4. Design
- Unique, innovative functionality
- Meaningful user experience
- Follow Apple's design guidelines
- No copying existing apps

### 5. Legal & Privacy
- Explicit data collection consent
- Privacy policy compliance
- GDPR/international privacy laws
- Clear data usage explanations

## Key Overarching Principles
- "Customers should know what they're getting when they download or buy your app"
- Prioritize user safety, privacy, and experience
- Maintain transparency and ethical development practices
- The guidelines emphasize creating high-quality, user-focused apps that provide genuine value while protecting user interests

## Submission Checklist
- [ ] Provide demo account or demo mode
- [ ] Document all features in "Notes for Review"
- [ ] Ensure all links work (support, subscriptions)
- [ ] Test thoroughly on latest iOS
- [ ] Include privacy policy
- [ ] Prepare login credentials for review team

## Review Timeline
- 90% of submissions reviewed within 24 hours
- Bug fixes for existing apps won't be delayed unless safety/legal issues

## Common Rejection Reasons
- **App Completeness (40% of rejections)** - crashes, placeholder content, incomplete information
- Missing or broken functionality
- Inadequate privacy disclosures
- Misleading app descriptions
- Non-functional links or features

## Special Considerations for 2025
- Compliance with Apple's latest privacy policy is compulsory
- Certain app types (banking, healthcare, gambling, cryptocurrency, air travel) must be submitted by legal entities, not individual developers
- All new features must be described specifically in the Notes for Review section
- Backend services must be live and accessible during review

## Documentation Requirements
- All new features, functionality, and product changes must be described with specificity in the Notes for Review section
- Generic descriptions will be rejected
- Features must be accessible for review

## Privacy Requirements
- Tell Apple how you use people's data and why you need it
- Privacy is extremely important - follow all privacy rules
- Obtain explicit consent for data collection
- Provide clear explanations of data usage

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do:
- Thoroughly test on devices running the latest software
- Fix all bugs before submitting
- Make sure all subscription and support links work
- Provide accurate app descriptions and screenshots
- Follow Apple's design and interface guidelines

### Don't:
- Include hidden, dormant, or undocumented features
- Provide misleading descriptions or screenshots
- Use non-public APIs
- Include placeholder content
- Submit apps that crash or have major bugs
